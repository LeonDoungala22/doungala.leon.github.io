Abstract

This research investigates the application of artificial intelligence and machine learning methodologies for the characterization and prediction of diabetes mellitus. As one of the leading global health challenges, diabetes affects approximately 537 million adults worldwide, with projections suggesting this figure may reach 783 million by 2045. The substantial socioeconomic burden and healthcare implications necessitate innovative approaches for early detection and intervention.

The research integrates sophisticated machine learning, deep learning, generative AI, and natural language processing techniques to create predictive frameworks for diabetes risk identification. This interdisciplinary approach culminated in the development of Elyon, a Python Flask-based web application that provides healthcare professionals with practical tools for diabetes analysis and management.

Key findings demonstrated that carefully engineered AI models can achieve significant improvements in prediction accuracy compared to traditional clinical assessment methods. Through feature importance analysis, the research identified several previously underrepresented biomarkers and their complex interactions that contribute to diabetes risk stratification. The developed methodology establishes a robust framework for applying AI in diabetes risk assessment with potential for integration into clinical practice.

Introduction

Diabetes is a critical global health challenge affecting millions of lives worldwide. This Master of Science research in Computer Engineering and Automation explores how artificial intelligence can revolutionize diabetes prediction and management through innovative ML techniques and technologies.

Diabetes is a chronic disease that affects millions of people worldwide. It is on the rise, causing serious health problems, economic challenges and reduced quality of life. According to recent reports, the number of adults with diabetes has quadrupled since 1980, mainly due to an increase in type 2 diabetes. Early detection and good management of diabetes are essential to prevent serious complications such as heart disease, kidney failure and blindness. However, traditional methods of diagnosing diabetes often miss the disease in its early stages, leading to delays in treatment and worse health outcomes.

Recently, advances in artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) have shown great potential for improving healthcare. These technologies help in early diagnosis, personalised treatment and effective management of chronic diseases such as diabetes. AI and ML can analyse large amounts of data to find patterns that may not be obvious to doctors. Using AI and ML to predict and manage diabetes could significantly improve patient care by providing more accurate, timely and personalised treatments.

Research Objectives

The main objective of this Master of Science thesis is to explore and develop AI and ML techniques to help predict and understand diabetes. Specifically, this study aims to:

  • Build and evaluate machine learning models to predict diabetes based on clinical and demographic data
  • Explore the use of generative AI techniques to generate additional data for better model training and improved prediction accuracy
  • Use LangChain for natural language processing (NLP) to extract important information from unstructured medical records and improve diabetes management
  • Compare the effectiveness of different machine learning models and generative AI techniques to find the best approach for predicting and understanding diabetes
  • Add to the existing body of knowledge by showing how AI and ML can be practically applied in healthcare, with a focus on diabetes

Methodology & Models

A multi-model comparison approach was employed to identify the most effective AI techniques for diabetes prediction, with particular focus on ensemble learning methods and deep neural networks.

This research employed a systematic methodology that integrates sophisticated feature engineering, robust model development, and comprehensive validation protocols to create predictive frameworks capable of identifying diabetes risk with high precision. A diverse array of machine learning algorithms was rigorously evaluated to determine optimal techniques for diabetes prediction using multidimensional clinical and demographic data.

Decision Trees & Random Forests

Implementation of tree-based algorithms with ensemble methods to improve prediction accuracy and identify key features contributing to diabetes risk assessment. These models provided excellent interpretability of results.

Neural Networks

Development of deep learning architectures with multiple hidden layers to capture complex non-linear relationships in patient data. Custom architectures were designed to handle both structured and unstructured medical information.

Generative Models

Implementation of GANs and VAEs to address data scarcity challenges by generating synthetic patient records that maintain statistical properties of real data while enhancing privacy protection.

LangChain & RAG Framework

Integration of advanced NLP capabilities for processing unstructured medical narratives, combining retrieval-based approaches with generative methods to provide contextually relevant insights.

Model Selection & Evaluation

Model selection was conducted through a rigorous cross-validation process with performance metrics including accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, and AUC-ROC. The validation strategy employed a stratified k-fold approach to ensure balanced representation of diabetic and non-diabetic cases across training and testing sets. Special attention was given to model calibration to ensure reliable probability estimates for clinical decision support.
